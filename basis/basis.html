Recontracted basis sets with spin-free and spin-orbit relativistic Hamiltonian

The relativistic basis sets are recontracted correlation-consistent and Dyall's basis sets with spin-free exact two-component theory in its one-electron variant (the SFX2C-1e scheme) and X2C with atomic-mean-field spin-orbit integral (the X2CAMF scheme).

Take 2p orbitals of a heavy atom as an example. In non-relativistic and scalar-relativistic calculations, the three (six with spin) 2p orbitals are degenerate. They share the same set of contraction coefficients. In contrast, in calculations with spin-orbit interaction included at mean-field level, the two 2 p 1 / 2 and four 2 p 3 / 2 spinors are energitically and spatially different. As a result, they are separately contracted in the contracted spin-orbit basis sets.

To use the spin-orbit contracted basis sets, you must have a program with j-adapted contraction feature. This is supported by CFOUR and PySCF. Please cite the original publications of correlation-consistent or Dyall's basis sets as well as the new contraction scheme paper.

All the relativistic contracted basis sets in CFOUR format can be downloaded here.

For PySCF users, the basis sets in CFOUR format can be accessed using python external library socutils. An example calculation can be found here.
