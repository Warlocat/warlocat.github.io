Particle-particle RPA for DEA/DIP states

The so-called random phase approximation (RPA) method in quantum chemistry is usually referring to the particle-hole RPA (ph-RPA), a method to describe either the electron correlation or the excited states. The ph-RPA for excited states is actually the same method as the LR TDHF methods.

The particle-particle RPA (pp-RPA) is another method in RPA family to describe the electron correlation or double electron attachment (DEA) and double ionization potential (DIP) states. People have shown that the RPA for electron correlation is strongly related to the coupled-cluster methods. In this sense, one can treat ph-RPA as an approximated EOM-CCSD for excitation energy and pp-RPA as an approximated EOM-CCSD for DEA and DIP. We will focus on the pp-RPA for DEA/DIP states in this note.

As we have shown, the ph-RPA can be derived from the (particle-hole) linear response function. Similarly, we can define the independent particle particle-particle (and hole-hole) linear response function as \[ \chi(x_1,x_2,x_1',x_2',\tau) = -i G(x_1,x_2') G(x_2,x_1') \]
